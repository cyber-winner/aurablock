# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated:** June 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how your information is handled when you use the AuraBlock software, including the local DNS resolver, the web dashboard, and the browser extension ("AuraBlock", "we", "us", or "our"). 

AuraBlock was built from the ground up with a privacy-first philosophy. **We do not collect, store, or sell your personal data.** 

Please read this policy to understand exactly how data flows within your own system when you use AuraBlock.

## 1. Information We Do NOT Collect

Because AuraBlock is software that you host on your own local hardware, we (the developers and maintainers) **never** have access to your data. Specifically, we DO NOT collect:
* Your browsing history.
* Your DNS queries.
* Your IP address or location data.
* Telemetry, analytics, or usage statistics regarding how you use the software.
* Personally Identifiable Information (PII) such as names, email addresses, or payment details.

There are no "phone home" mechanics built into the AuraBlock core engine or its associated browser extension. 

## 2. How Data is Handled Locally on Your Device

While we do not collect your data, the AuraBlock software does process and store data locally on your machine to function properly.

### 2.1 DNS Queries and Local Database
When AuraBlock operates as your system-wide DNS resolver, every DNS request your device makes passes through the AuraBlock engine. 
* **Processing:** The engine reads the requested domain to match it against your blocklists.
* **Storage:** By default, AuraBlock logs these queries to a local SQLite database (`backend/aurablock.db`) stored securely on your machine. This database is used exclusively to render the charts, statistics, and live query stream in your local Web Dashboard. 
* **Control:** You have full ownership of this file. You can delete it, clear the logs via the dashboard, or run the software without logging if you choose to modify the source code.

### 2.2 Third-Party Blocklists
AuraBlock downloads publicly available blocklists (such as curated community filters) to know what to block. 
* Your IP address may be exposed to the servers hosting these blocklists (e.g., GitHub) when AuraBlock periodically fetches updates. 

### 2.3 Upstream DNS Providers
AuraBlock is a DNS forwarder. If a domain is not blocked, the request must be sent to an upstream DNS provider (such as your ISP or a public DNS resolver) to be resolved.
* **Third-Party Privacy:** Once AuraBlock forwards a query to an upstream provider, that provider's privacy policy applies. We highly recommend configuring AuraBlock to use privacy-respecting upstream DNS servers.

### 2.4 Browser Extension
The AuraBlock Shield extension uses the Declarative Net Request (DNR) API. 
* It intercepts network requests locally within your browser to block ads. 
* It does not transmit your browsing habits to us or any remote server. 
* Any preferences saved by the extension are stored locally using standard browser storage APIs.

## 3. Data Security

The data processed by AuraBlock never leaves your device unless you explicitly route it (such as sending unresolved DNS queries to an upstream server). 
* The Web Dashboard is served locally on port `8082` (by default) and is meant to be accessed only from the `localhost`. 
* We strongly advise against exposing the AuraBlock dashboard or API directly to the public internet without implementing robust reverse-proxy authentication, as it could expose your local browsing habits to unauthorized parties.

## 4. Children's Privacy

AuraBlock does not knowingly process any data, as all processing is local. Parents and guardians may use AuraBlock to filter content on their local network or devices, in which case the local administrator holds full responsibility for the data generated by users on that network, including children.

## 5. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the software's architecture or features. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy in this repository.

You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes to this Privacy Policy are effective when they are published here.

## 6. Contact Us

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or how your local data is handled, please open an issue in our GitHub repository.
